On-chain treasuries are glass houses. The moment a fund rebalances, buys, or pays out, everyone sees it, and bots front-run the trade while competitors copy the strategy. Sandwich attacks alone drained over two hundred and eighty nine million dollars in the first half of twenty twenty five. CTIN is a confidential execution layer that fixes this without forking any protocol. The operator opens a batch for institutions to join. An institution composes an intent, buy four hundred ETH, but the amount is encrypted on the device before it ever reaches the chain. Only an opaque handle is submitted, and only the owner can decrypt their own position. On the public explorer, everyone sees a single netted footprint, never who traded, or how much. And when compliance demands it, the institution grants a chosen auditor scoped, revocable disclosure, confidential to the public, readable only by the people you authorize. Confidential treasury execution, running on your existing Safe. This is CTIN.
